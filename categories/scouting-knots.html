Scouting & Camping Knots

Scouting knots build practical outdoor skills used in camp construction, pioneering, and everyday wilderness problem-solving. These are the classic knots taught in every scouting program worldwide.

Advertisement

Square Knot

Easy

Basic joining knot for bandages, packages, and joining equal-diameter rope or cord.

Bowline

Intermediate

Forms a fixed loop for rescue, climbing, and camp rigging. The most important knot every scout must master.

Clove Hitch

Easy

Foundation of all lashing — start and finish every lashing with a clove hitch.

Taut Line Hitch

Intermediate

Adjustable knot for tent guylines — slide to tension, grips under load. The essential camping knot.

Timber Hitch

Easy

Grips a log for dragging or hoisting. The harder you pull, the tighter it holds. Self-releasing.

Sheepshank

Intermediate

Temporarily shortens a rope without cutting — also used to bypass a damaged section.

Square Lashing

Intermediate

Joins two poles at right angles for pioneering structures. The fundamental camp construction skill.

Trucker's Hitch

Intermediate

3:1 mechanical advantage for tensioning guylines and cargo. Locks off with two half hitches.

Half Hitch

Easy

The simplest hitch — used in pairs to finish countless knot applications.
