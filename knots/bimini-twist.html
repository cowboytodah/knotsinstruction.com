How to Tie a Bimini Twist

The Bimini twist creates a 100% strength double-line loop — the gold standard for big game fishing leaders and tournament angling where no knot strength can be sacrificed.

Written Instructions — Bimini Twist

  1. Double the line Double 3–4 feet of line, put foot through loop end to hold open.
  2. 20 twists Hold both strands and twist them around each other 20 times. Count carefully.
  3. Roll tag end over twists Spread loop legs, maintain tension — guide tag end rolling back over the twisted barrel.
  4. Lock off with half hitches Half hitch around one leg, then two half hitches around both legs.
  5. Finished loop 100% strength double-line loop. Attach leaders via Albright or loop-to-loop.

Tips for Tying a Bimini Twist

  • 20 twists is the minimum for 20–50 lb line. Use 15 twists for heavier line over 80 lb.
  • Maintaining even tension on the loop throughout the rolling step is the critical variable.
  • Have a partner hold the loop end on a cleat or your foot for solo tying.
  • The Bimini is the required knot for IGFA record applications.
